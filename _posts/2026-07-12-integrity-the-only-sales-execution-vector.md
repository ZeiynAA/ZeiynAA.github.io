# Only Integrity Matters in Sales



Throughout your career in sales, you've heard of multiple sales framework methodologies, from MEDDIC to SPIN. There have been multiple ways to qualify and generate confidence in a B2B sales cycle. Over time, these frameworks have established a stable foundation for salespeople to forecast deal velocity in a repeatable, consistent manner. What are the best salespeople, from B2C to B2B, from Mid-Market through to Enterprise, all saying are the key to their success? What the top performers have always had is the ability to build and sustain deep & meaningful relationships.

This article seeks to reveal how those relationships are formed and sustained over time. It all comes from a cornerstone mindset of integrity. That is the catalyst for all relationships. What I seek to reveal to you is where integrity manifests, how it impacts your life and how it resonates in your personal/professional life to build deep trusting relationships. 

Before I continue, I want to make one thing clear. As a top performer, my main objective as a salesperson is only one. To become a trusted partner to my clients. The secondary objective is to generate revenue; even then, I would do so only when it would be an advantageous commercial structure for everyone involved. I'd urge you to consider keeping an open mind as you continue. 

Working in Strategic Enterprise B2B SaaS sales is less about getting the right deal and more about establishing a consistent framework that positions you as the first choice when new projects are initiated. To be considered as that first choice, you must be the trusted partner. There is no other way to set up a consistent pipeline that self-feeds if your customers do not bring the deals to you on a silver platter. The only way this works consistently is for your customers to approach you as their strategic partner. 

All of this being said. Let's dive into the four agreements that shape the worldview necessary for this approach.

### The Four Agreements



1. **Be Impeccable With Your Word**
  1. Speak with integrity.
  2. Say only what you mean.
  3. Avoid using the word to speak against yourself or to gossip about others.
  4. Use the power of your word in the direction of truth & love.
2. **Don't Take Anything Personally**
  1. Nothing others do is because of you.
  2. What others say and do reflects their own reality.
  3. When you are immune to the opinions and actions of others, you won't be the victim of needless suffering.
3. **Don't Make Assumptions**
  1. Find the courage to ask questions and to express what you really want.
  2. Communicate with others as clearly as you can to avoid misunderstandings and drama.
4. **Always Do Your Best**
  1. Your best changes from moment to moment.
  2. Under any circumstance, simply do your best, and you will avoid self-judgement, self-abuse and regret.
